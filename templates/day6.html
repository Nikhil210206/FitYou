Day 6: Active Recovery & Stretch

Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)

  • 2-3 minutes Light Cardio (e.g., gentle marching in place, arm circles)
  • 2-3 minutes Dynamic Stretches (e.g., Cat-Cow, gentle torso twists, leg swings)

Active Recovery & Stretch Exercises (40 minutes)

Cat-Cow Stretch

Sets: 3

Reps: 10-12 controlled movements

Tip: Synchronize movement with breath for spinal mobility.

Bird-Dog

Sets: 3

Reps: 10-12 per side

Tip: Maintain a flat back, move slowly to focus on core stability.

Child's Pose (Balasana)

Sets: 3

Hold: 30-60 seconds

Tip: Relax into the stretch, focusing on deep breaths to release tension.

Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

Sets: 3

Hold: 30-60 seconds

Tip: Press heels towards the mat, lengthen the spine, slight bend in knees if hamstrings are tight.

Standing Hamstring Stretch

Sets: 3

Hold: 30 seconds per leg

Tip: Keep a straight back, hinge from the hips, avoid rounding your spine.

Standing Quad Stretch

Sets: 3

Hold: 30 seconds per leg

Tip: Keep knees together, gently pull your heel towards your glute.

Figure-Four Stretch (Supine)

Sets: 3

Hold: 30 seconds per side

Tip: Lie on your back, cross one ankle over the opposite knee, gently pull the thigh towards your chest.

Seated Thoracic Spine Rotation

Sets: 3

Reps: 10-12 per side

Tip: Sit tall, twist from your upper back, keep hips stable.

Side Bend Stretch (Standing)

Sets: 3

Hold: 30 seconds per side

Tip: Reach overhead and gently lean to the side, feeling the stretch along your torso.

Wrist and Ankle Circles

Sets: 3

Reps: 10-15 circles each direction

Tip: Perform slowly and controlled to improve joint mobility.

Cool Down (5-10 minutes)

Perform static stretches for major muscle groups, holding each for 20-30 seconds.

  • Hamstring Stretch
  • Quad Stretch
  • Chest Stretch
  • Tricep Stretch
  • Shoulder Stretch

Focus on deep, calming breaths to aid muscle recovery and relaxation.

Additional Tips for Active Recovery

  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day to support recovery and flexibility.
  • Mind-Body Connection: Focus on your breathing and how your body feels during each stretch.
  • Gentle Movement: Listen to your body and avoid pushing into pain. The goal is gentle movement and increased range of motion.
  • Consistency: Regular active recovery and stretching can significantly improve overall flexibility and reduce muscle soreness.
  • Proprioception: Pay attention to your body's position in space to improve balance and coordination.
